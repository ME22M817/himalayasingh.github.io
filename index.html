Role of Microstructure on Stress-Diffusion Interactions in Li-Ion Battery Electrode

Simulated stress diffusion coupling during lithiation and delithiation using multiphysics FEM, and analysed the effects of grain size, boundary thickness, and orientation on stress evolution and failure mechanisms.

  • Studied microstructural influence on stress localisation using grain size, grain boundary thickness, and orientation parameters.
  • Analysed von Mises stress response under varying partial molar volume (ω) and diffusion-driven gradients.
  • Investigated high-frequency time-dependent boundary conditions and their impact on concentration gradients and stress build-up.

Conclusion: Smaller grain sizes reduce von Mises stress and stress localization due to lower gradients and better strain accommodation, while larger grains show higher stresses. Von Mises stress generally increases with partial molar volume (ω), though localized reductions may occur, and high frequency time dependent boundary conditions induce high stresses due to steep concentration gradients.
